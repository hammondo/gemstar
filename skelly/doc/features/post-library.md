The library approach makes a lot of sense and actually aligns well with what's already in the todo (items 2 and 4 — using Fresha signals to inform decisions). Here's my thinking:

# Why it works well:

- Low-pressure for the owner — content is ready, they just pick and schedule
- Content quality stays high since posts aren't rushed
- Naturally pairs with Fresha signals (todo #4) — low booking signal for a service = good time to pull a post from that service's library

# How the library could work:

- Posts generated ahead of time per service, stored with status: draft | scheduled | posted
- Each entry has the post copy, image(s), and maybe variant tags (e.g. "promotional", "educational", "seasonal")
- Owner browses the library, picks one, sets a date/time — done

# What makes it even better:

- Generate several variations per service so there's always fresh content to choose from
- Track which posts have been used so they don't repeat
- The AI generates in bulk during quieter periods, human schedules on demand

# The tradeoff to consider:

- Someone still needs to trigger generation. You'd want enough posts in the library at any time that it never feels empty — maybe a low-stock warning when a service's library is running thin.
- This is a cleaner model than full automation tbh. The owner stays in control of timing without having to think about content.
